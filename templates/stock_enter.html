3 Chinese Stocks to Buy Now — Including Alibaba

Analyst Yijun Wang Category Internet
Introduction Analysis

Introduction

If you like risk, Chinese stocks are a good market to be in. As Groucho Marx said nearly a century ago about Florida real estate, “You can even get stucco. Oh, how you can you get stuck-o.”

The ongoing trade war, and the possibility of peace, is making Chinese stocks very volatile. They can be pummeled one week, then surge the next, and the government’s reaction to these gyrations is watched even more than in the U.S.

Our Matt McCall recommended three good Chinese stocks earlier this month, but there are others you should look at in building a portfolio. I would focus on technology and consumer marketing, as that’s where the growth is. And I would focus on companies of the largest size because that’s where your protection is.

Alibaba (BABA)

Alibaba Group Holding (NASDAQ:BABA) may be the best known Chinese stock because of executive chairman Jack Ma, who plans to retire next year. The man to watch here is his replacement, Daniel Zhang, who has re-focused the company on physical infrastructure, making it a major player in Chinese shopping malls and retail centers. This has had a negative impact on the company’s profitability, but it still drew 10% of revenue to the net income line for the June quarter, the most recent one reported. The company is expected to next report earnings Nov. 2, with analysts expecting about $2.1 billion in earnings, 82 cents per share, on revenue of $12.96 billion, against $7.87 billion a year ago.
